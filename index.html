Namaste , i am LIN .

Life is a journey. And after years’ of searching and learning, now I am here, with my best companion, YOGA, to explore and share a healthier and happier way.

about me

My story with yoga begins in the summer of 2013, after I heard so many times from my friends about this “fashion” thing, and finally had a try when I got a lot of free time after I left my company working as a sales in international trade. And just after the first class, I fell in love with yoga, as I found that not only I felt lighter in my body, but also there was some change in my mind too. I felt calmer and happier, which I never expected. So I kept practicing in that center for a month, and continued on my own, enjoying the stretches and movements and the seed of peace and happiness growing in my heart.

My life turned to something challenge since my college time. My family members fell sick one after another. And my grandmother, my aunt, and my grandfather, all got cancer, and then my father too. He was diagnosed with cancer in the esophagus, 4th stage. And after suffering and fighting for seven months, he passed away, in March 2014. Thinking of what had happened to me in those several years, and thinking of myself, who also suffered a lot from diseases since childhood. Never than any time more deeply I realized how important body health is. After struggling in those sleepless nights due to the pain caused by the great loss of my father, I then started to question myself, the meaning of life. And looking at my life which at that time I could even see the rest of it, I clearly heard a voice from deep in my heart, that I wanna a change. Though not knowing what might come, on July 29th, 2014, I got on the first flight trip in my life, and traveled thousands of miles away to India, for yoga.

Life in India was interesting and frustrating too. I loved the colorful culture, and the religions, and the people I met, stories I heard, and together with the learning of yoga, all these things opened the gate to another world for me. And for the first time in my life, I got to know that there is another world called spirituality. And since the day I made the decision to come to India, I told myself that I wanna a change. And after a couple of years there, I knew I got it, and it is something more than I could imagine or expect. My disease which not only one doctor advised me that it is incurable, was cured. And the medicine which I thought I might have to take for the rest of my life, was stopped. And more important, things I cared I care no more, things I desired I desire no more. And for years I was searching for happiness, I stopped searching, as I got to know that it is nowhere else to search but into my own heart.

I spent four years in India, and this four years’ journey, which I came for yoga, health and happiness, and during which I laughed, cried, hesitated, and wandered, was ended with appreciation, and a heart open to another journey that is coming yet unknown. Though there is still a lot to learn, it’s still a long journey to go, but knowing deeply how yoga has changed my life, and became a part of life, I think it’s time for me to start teaching, sharing my knowledge and understanding of yoga, or at least introducing a healthier and happier way, to all the people on this journey called life.
